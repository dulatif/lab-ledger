Based on the `datastructures-and-algorithms.pdf` roadmap, here is a structured syllabus divided into three proficiency levels. This curriculum is designed to take you from basic programming constructs to advanced algorithmic problem-solving.

### **Level 1: Beginner (Foundations & Linear Structures)**

**Goal:** Master the syntax of your chosen language, understand how to measure code efficiency, and implement fundamental linear data structures.

**Module 1: Language & Fundamentals**

- **Setup:** "Pick a Language" (C++, Java, Python, Go, etc.) and master "Programming Fundamentals".

- **Core Concepts:** "Language Syntax" , "Control Structures" , "Functions" , and "OOP Basics".

- **Theory:** Answering "What are Data Structures?" and "Why are Data Structures Important?".

**Module 2: Complexity Analysis**

- **Measurement:** Understanding "Algorithmic Complexity" and "Time vs Space Complexity".

- **Notation:** Mastering "Asymptotic Notation" and "Big-O Notation". \* **Runtimes:** Recognizing "Common Runtimes" such as Constant , Logarithmic , Linear , Polynomial , Exponential , and Factorial.

**Module 3: Basic Data Structures**

- **Linear:** Working with "Array" and "Linked Lists".

- **LIFO/FIFO:** Implementing "Queues" and "Stacks".

- **Key-Value:** Understanding "Hash Tables".

- **Basic Search:** Implementing "Linear Search" and "Binary Search".

---

### **Level 2: Intermediate (Non-Linear Structures & Sorting)**

**Goal:** Implement complex hierarchical structures (Trees, Graphs) and master efficient sorting algorithms.

**Module 4: Sorting Algorithms**

- **Elementary Sorts:** "Bubble Sort" , "Insertion Sort" , and "Selection Sort".

- **Efficient Sorts:** "Merge Sort" , "Quick Sort" , and "Heap Sort".

**Module 5: Tree Data Structures**

- **Core Trees:** "Binary Trees" and "Binary Search Trees" (BST). \* **Heaps:** Understanding the "Heap" data structure.

- **Traversal:** Mastering "Tree Traversal" : "In-Order Traversal" , "Pre-Order Traversal" , and "Post-Order Traversal".

**Module 6: Graph Data Structures**

- **Definitions:** "Directed Graph" vs "Undirected Graph".

- **Traversal:** "Breadth First Search" (BFS) and "Depth First Search" (DFS).

- **Shortest Path:** "Dijkstra's Algorithm" , "Bellman-Ford Algoritm" , and the "A\* Algorithm".

---

### **Level 3: Advanced (Optimization, Patterns & Indexing)**

**Goal:** Solve complex competitive programming problems using advanced structures and dynamic programming patterns.

**Module 7: Advanced & Complex Structures**

- **Self-Balancing Trees:** "AVL Trees" , "B-Trees" , "2-3 Trees" , and "B/B+ Trees".

- **Range Queries:** "Segment Trees" and "Fenwick Trees".

- **String Structures:** "Trie" , "Suffix Trees and Arrays".

- **Sets:** "Disjoint Set (Union-Find)".

- **Indexing:** "Linear" vs "Tree-Based" Indexing and "ISAM".

**Module 8: Graph Optimization**

- **Spanning Trees:** "Minimum Spanning Tree" using "Prim's Algorithm" and "Kruskal's Algorithm".

**Module 9: Problem Solving Techniques**

- **Paradigms:** "Brute Force" , "Backtracking" , "Greedy Algorithms" , "Divide and Conquer" , and "Dynamic Programming".

- **Pointers:** "Two Pointer Technique" , "Fast and Slow Pointers" , and "Sliding Window Technique".

- **Specific Patterns:** "Island traversal" , "Two Heaps" , "Merge Intervals" , "Cyclic Sort" , and "Kth Element".

- **Practice:** Application on platforms like "Leetcode" and "Edabit".
